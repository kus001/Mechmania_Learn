    Always verify and be aware of voltage, polarity, and

Before starting to design circuits or wiring robots, it is important to understand the basic measurements 
used to describe electricity to know what you are working with.

The 3 basic measurements are:

- Voltage
- Current
- Resistance

We will dive into each of these measurements.

## Voltage

Voltage is the electrical push or force that drives current through a circuit. It is measured in **Volts (V)**.

It provides the push that drives current through a circuit.

:::danger
Feeding higher voltage into a component than it is rated for will **damage** the component.
:::

## Current

Current is the rate at which electric charge flows through a circuit. It is measured in **Amperes or Amps (A)**.
It measures how much electric charge flows through the circuit each second.

:::note
Components draw current based on their electrical load. Your battery/power supply must be able to safely supply the 
current your system demands.
:::

## Resistance

Resistance opposes the flow of electric current through a circuit. It is measured in **Ohms (Ω)**.
It restricts how much current can pass through the circuit. 

## Ohm's Law

Ohm's Law connects Voltage, Current, and Resistance into a single formula. 
It is the most fundamental equation in electronics:

`V = I × R`

By rearranging this formula, you can calculate any missing value in your circuit:

- **Calculate Voltage:** `V = I × R`
- **Calculate Current:** `I = V / R`
- **Calculate Resistance:** `R = V / I`

## Example

A motor is connected to a **12V battery** and has a resistance of **6Ω**.

Using Ohm's Law:

`I = V / R`

`I = 12V / 6Ω`

`I = 2A`

The motor draws **2 Amps** of current.
